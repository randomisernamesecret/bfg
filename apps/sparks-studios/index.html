Sparks Studios

A creative playground where kids draw, write, and compose.

Download on the App Store

Screen Time Does Not Have to Be Passive

The screen time debate has been framed wrong. The real question is not "how many hours" but "doing what." A child watching thirty minutes of autoplay videos on YouTube is having a fundamentally different experience from a child who spends thirty minutes drawing a picture, writing a short story, or composing a melody. One is consumption. The other is creation. The screen is the same, but the cognitive engagement is not even close.

Parents know this intuitively, which is why they look for creative apps. But the options are frustrating. Many so-called creative apps for kids are ad-supported, which means a six-year-old is shown advertisements between drawing sessions. Others require accounts, which means an email address and personal data flowing to a server somewhere. Some upload children's creations to the cloud for "sharing" features that no parent asked for and no child needs.

Then there is the age problem. A drawing app designed for a four-year-old is boring for a ten-year-old. A story builder aimed at eleven-year-olds is overwhelming for a five-year-old. Parents end up cycling through apps as their child grows, never finding one that adapts to where the child actually is.

How Sparks Studios Addresses This

Sparks Studios is a creative toolkit built around three activities: drawing, story writing, and music composition. Each one is powered by on-device AI that assists without taking over. In the drawing canvas, a child can sketch freely or get gentle prompts and suggestions. In the story builder, AI helps with story structure and vocabulary — offering words or plot ideas when a child gets stuck, rather than writing the story for them. The music composer lets children create melodies by placing notes and hearing them played back, with AI suggesting harmonies or rhythmic patterns they can accept or ignore.

All AI processing happens on the device itself. Nothing is uploaded to any server. There are no ads, no analytics SDKs, no tracking pixels, and no account creation. A child opens the app and starts creating immediately. This is a deliberate architectural decision, not a marketing claim — there is literally no server to send data to.

The app is age-adapted across three tiers: 4-6, 7-9, and 10-12. The interface, prompts, vocabulary, and complexity all adjust based on the selected age range. A four-year-old sees large, colourful controls and simple prompts. A ten-year-old gets more sophisticated tools and richer creative options. This means one app works across the primary school years without needing to be replaced as your child grows.

Parental controls are built in. You can set session time limits, see what your child has created, and adjust the age tier. There is no social sharing, no public gallery, no way for a child to accidentally publish their work or interact with strangers. Everything stays on the device, in the family.

Key Features

  • Drawing canvas — freeform sketching with AI-powered prompts and suggestions
  • Story builder — write stories with AI assistance for structure and vocabulary
  • Music composer — create melodies with harmony and rhythm suggestions
  • On-device AI — nothing uploaded, no account required
  • Age-adapted — three tiers (4-6, 7-9, 10-12) with appropriate interfaces
  • Parental controls — time limits, creation gallery, no social features
