What It Does

Eight Graded Levels

From your first C to advanced repertoire. Each grade ends with an exam-style assessment that gates the next, so progression feels earned rather than skipped.

Falling-Note Practice

Notes fall toward a hit line; you play the corresponding key. Tempo slows down without changing pitch, hands can be isolated, and a metronome rides along.

Real Music Theory

Reading the staff, intervals, scales, key signatures, dynamics, articulation. Theory is folded into lessons rather than buried in a separate manual.

Sight-Reading and Ear Training

Treble and bass clef sight-read drills, ear-copy challenges that play a phrase and ask you to repeat it, mental rehearsal exercises that strengthen audiation.

Scales, Chords, and Improv

Scale tests, chord drills, and free-improvisation sessions inside a chosen scale. The course teaches both the literacy and the language of piano.

26 Public-Domain Pieces

From Bach minuets to Beethoven sonatas, sourced from the Mutopia Project. Ranged from absolute beginner to virtuoso, with grand-piano renders.

Bring Your Own MIDI

Import any .mid or .midi file from the Files app. Keystrike parses it into a falling-note chart and plays it back so you can practise anything you can find or write.

Acoustic-Piano Mode

No MIDI keyboard? Keystrike listens through the microphone for upright and grand pianos and converts each detected pitch into a note. Calibration is built in.

Free Play and Recording

An open keyboard for improvisation. Record what you play, save it, and export the recording as a standard MIDI file.

Streaks, XP, Achievements

Daily-streak tracking, XP earned per lesson, exam-pass animations, and a weak-spot tracker that flags pitch classes you keep missing so practice targets the right gaps.

Privacy & Data

  • No account required. No sign-up, no email, no password.
  • Progress, recordings, and imported MIDI files stay on the iPad or Mac.
  • Microphone use (acoustic-piano mode) is on-device only — audio never leaves the device.
  • No ads, no tracking, no analytics, no third-party SDKs.
  • Notifications are optional and used only for the daily-practice reminder.

Who It Is For

Keystrike is for the absolute beginner who has never sat at a piano, the returner who studied as a child and wants a structured way back, and the intermediate self-taught player who has been learning songs by ear and now wants to read the page. It works equally well as a daily companion between lessons with a teacher and as a stand-alone course.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a MIDI keyboard?

No. Use a USB or Bluetooth MIDI keyboard if you have one, an acoustic piano with the microphone, or the on-screen keyboard. Many beginners start without any hardware at all.

How many grades are there?

Eight, plus a Grade 0 onboarding for absolute beginners. Each grade has a multi-step lesson sequence followed by an exam.

Is there an Android version?

No. Keystrike is iPad and Mac only. The Mac version uses Mac Catalyst and supports the same MIDI keyboards.

Will my progress sync between iPad and Mac?

Not at launch. Keystrike is designed around the principle that your data does not leave the device, which means cross-device sync would have to be opt-in and we have not built it yet. For now, pick the device you do most of your practising on.

Can I use my own sheet music?

You cannot import PDFs at launch. You can import MIDI files (.mid, .midi) and Keystrike will parse them into a playable chart automatically.

Is it for children or adults?

Both. The pacing of the early grades is gentle enough for a focused eight-year-old; the depth of the later grades is enough for adults working toward exam-grade repertoire.
