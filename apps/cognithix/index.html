The 11+ Exam Gap

The 11+ exam is not one test — it is four. Grammar schools and selective schools across England increasingly test English, Verbal Reasoning, Non-Verbal Reasoning, and the Language Aptitude Test (LAT). But most preparation apps only cover one or two of these areas. So parents end up cobbling together a patchwork: one app for English comprehension, a workbook for verbal reasoning, printed PDFs for non-verbal reasoning, and almost nothing for the LAT because so few resources exist.

This is expensive and chaotic. Individual workbooks cost between five and fifteen pounds each. Tutoring runs forty to sixty pounds per hour. And even with all that spending, coverage is inconsistent — a child might be well-prepared for English but barely exposed to the pattern recognition questions in NVR, or comfortable with maths-adjacent VR questions but blindsided by the LAT on exam day.

The LAT is a particular blind spot. Introduced by CEM and used by an increasing number of schools, it tests a child's ability to learn and apply rules from an unfamiliar constructed language. Very few practice materials exist for it, and most parents do not even know it will appear on the test until weeks before the exam.

How Cognithix Addresses This

Cognithix is built specifically to cover all four tested areas in a single app. English comprehension, Verbal Reasoning, Non-Verbal Reasoning, and the Language Aptitude Test are all included, with questions generated on-device so the supply is effectively unlimited. A child cannot simply memorise answers by repeating the same problem set — each session generates fresh material.

The difficulty adapts to each child individually. If your child breezes through antonym questions but struggles with code-breaking in Verbal Reasoning, Cognithix adjusts. It raises the difficulty on what they find easy (so they stay challenged) and provides more practice at the right level for areas where they need it (so they build confidence rather than frustration).

A parent dashboard shows mastery across all four subjects. You can see at a glance which areas are strong, which need more work, and how accuracy has changed over time. This matters because parents typically have limited preparation time — knowing exactly where to focus makes a significant difference.

To keep younger children motivated through what can feel like months of repetitive practice, Cognithix includes a virtual pet that grows and evolves based on consistent effort. It rewards showing up and trying, not just getting answers right — a deliberate design choice to encourage a growth mindset during a stressful period.

Key Features

  • All 4 tested areas — English, Verbal Reasoning, Non-Verbal Reasoning, and the LAT
  • Adaptive difficulty — adjusts to each child's accuracy in real time
  • Parent dashboard — mastery tracking across all subjects
  • Virtual pet — motivates consistent practice without gamifying learning
  • On-device generation — unlimited fresh questions, no internet required
