Source-linked financial data you can actually verify.

Download on the App Store

The Problem with Financial Data

A Bloomberg terminal costs around $25,000 a year. For professional fund managers, that is a line item. For everyone else, it is a non-starter. So most retail investors turn to free alternatives — apps and websites that surface earnings figures, revenue numbers, balance sheet data. The problem is that almost none of them tell you where the data comes from.

You see a revenue number. Is it from the latest 10-K filing? A preliminary estimate? A third-party model? There is no way to tell. You are making decisions based on numbers you cannot verify, sourced from pipelines you cannot inspect.

The irony is that most of this data originates from public filings. The SEC makes 10-K, 10-Q, and 8-K filings freely available through EDGAR. The Federal Reserve publishes economic data through FRED. These are authoritative, primary sources — but navigating them on a phone is an exercise in frustration. They were built for desktop browsers and institutional workflows, not for someone checking a stock on the train.

How Kanesh Solves This

Kanesh takes a different approach: every financial number in the app is source-tagged. Revenue, net income, EPS, debt ratios — each data point links back to the specific SEC filing, FRED series, or data provider it came from. You can tap any number and see exactly where it originated. No black boxes, no mystery aggregations.

Instead of learning financial query languages or navigating complex filing structures, you can ask questions in plain English. "What was Apple's gross margin last quarter?" or "Show me Tesla's debt-to-equity ratio over 5 years." The on-device AI processes your query entirely on your phone — your questions never leave the device, and no account is required.

Kanesh covers companies across 53 countries through an interactive 3D globe interface, so you are not limited to US equities. And with the built-in Congress trading tracker, you can see which stocks members of the US Congress are buying and selling — data sourced directly from mandatory disclosure filings.

Key Features

  • Source-tagged financials — every number linked to its original filing or data source
  • Natural language queries — ask questions in plain English, processed on-device
  • 53 countries — global equity coverage via interactive 3D globe
  • Congress trading tracker — see what US politicians are buying and selling
  • On-device AI — no data leaves your phone, no account needed
